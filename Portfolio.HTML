Final Year Project

Common Skin Disease Diagnosis and Prediction.
focuses on the integration of computer technology
into the healthcare industry, particularly in the field
of dermatology.
Here's a concise overview of Our project:

Project Overview:

This project focuses on leveraging machine learning and deep learning techniques, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), MobileNet, and Residual Neural Network (ResNet), to improve the diagnosis and prediction of common skin diseases.

Skin diseases present unique challenges due to their diverse nature, influenced by genetics and environmental factors.
Accurate and timely diagnosis is crucial, as some skin disorders can be chronic or life-threatening if not detected early.

The project aims to enhance dermatology by creating a computer-aided skin disease diagnosis model.
This model helps dermatologists make objective and reliable initial diagnoses, leading to better patient care and minimizing the risk of side effects from incorrect treatments.

The challenges in skin disease diagnosis include low contrast between lesions and normal skin, visual similarities between diseased and healthy areas, and variations in skin color and tone.

By utilizing deep learning techniques and image processing, the project analyzes skin images, removes noise, and extracts essential information for disease detection.
The ultimate goal is to provide doctors with a valuable tool for more accurate and efficient skin disease diagnosis, contributing to improved patient outcomes and well-being.

Our project addresses a critical need in the medical field and has the potential to make a significant impact in dermatology by enhancing the accuracy and efficiency of skin disease diagnosis.
